Chole Bhature

Approx. 500 calories per serving

Health Benefits: Chole (chickpeas) are a good source of protein and fiber. When paired with bhature (fried bread), it provides energy and satiety.


Ingredients:

  • 1 cup dried chickpeas (soaked overnight)
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • Bhature (fried bread) for serving

Process:

  1. Pressure cook soaked chickpeas until soft.
  2. In a pan, heat oil and add cumin seeds.
  3. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Cook until fragrant.
  5. Add tomato puree, turmeric powder, coriander powder, garam masala, and salt. Cook until oil separates.
  6. Add cooked chickpeas and water as needed. Simmer until desired consistency.
  7. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with bhature.

Paneer Tikka Masala

Approx. 400 calories per serving

Health Benefits: Paneer (cottage cheese) is a good source of protein and calcium. When combined with vegetables and spices, it provides essential nutrients.


Ingredients:

  • 250g paneer, cubed
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 capsicum, diced
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Process:

  1. Marinate paneer cubes with ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Let it sit for 30 minutes.
  2. Heat oil in a pan and add cumin seeds.
  3. Add chopped onions and sauté until translucent.
  4. Add capsicum and cook until slightly tender.
  5. Add tomato puree, garam masala, and salt. Cook until oil separates.
  6. Add marinated paneer cubes and simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  7. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with roti or rice.

Vegetable Biryani

Approx. 450 calories per serving

Health Benefits: Vegetable biryani is a nutritious one-pot meal with a combination of vegetables, rice, and spices. It provides carbohydrates, vitamins, and minerals.


Ingredients:

  • 1 cup basmati rice, soaked for 30 minutes
  • Assorted vegetables (carrots, beans, peas, cauliflower), chopped
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon biryani masala
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • Fresh coriander leaves and mint leaves for garnish

Process:

  1. Heat oil in a pressure cooker and add cumin seeds.
  2. Add sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste, chopped tomatoes, and spices. Cook until tomatoes are soft.
  4. Add chopped vegetables and sauté for 2-3 minutes.
  5. Drain soaked rice and add it to the cooker. Mix well.
  6. Add water (1:2 ratio of rice to water), cover the cooker, and cook until one whistle.
  7. Let the pressure release naturally. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and mint leaves. Serve hot with raita.

Palak Paneer

Approx. 350 calories per serving

Health Benefits: Palak (spinach) is rich in iron, calcium, and antioxidants. Paneer (cottage cheese) provides protein and calcium. This dish is nutritious and beneficial for overall health.


Ingredients:

  • 250g paneer, cubed
  • 1 bunch spinach leaves, washed and chopped
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cream for garnish

Process:

  1. Blanch spinach leaves in boiling water for 2 minutes. Drain and blend to a smooth paste.
  2. Heat oil in a pan and add cumin seeds.
  3. Add chopped onions and sauté until translucent.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook until fragrant.
  5. Add tomato puree, turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt. Cook until oil separates.
  6. Add spinach puree and cook for 5 minutes.
  7. Add paneer cubes and simmer for another 5 minutes.
  8. Garnish with fresh cream and serve hot with roti or rice.
